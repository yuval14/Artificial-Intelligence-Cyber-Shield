# AI Workforce

This page provides a curated collection of authoritative reports, data briefs, policy studies, and analytical tools concerning the artificial intelligence workforce. It covers the identification and measurement of AI development talent, education and training pipelines, public-sector and defense workforce challenges, and the broader effects of AI on jobs and skills.

> **Terminology note:** The specialized **AI development workforce** includes workers who directly design, train, fine-tune, scale, and deploy AI systems. This group should be distinguished from workers who use AI tools and workers whose tasks may be affected by AI-enabled automation or augmentation.

## Suggested Analytical Categories

The resources can be used across five complementary areas:

1. **Workforce definition and measurement:** Who should be classified as an AI worker, and how should demand and supply be measured?
2. **AI development capacity:** Which workers directly design, train, fine-tune, deploy, and maintain AI systems?
3. **Education and talent pipelines:** Which institutions, credentials, training programs, and career pathways can grow and diversify AI talent?
4. **Organizational and national readiness:** How effectively can governments, defense organizations, and industry identify, recruit, retain, and deploy AI talent?
5. **Labor-market transformation:** Which occupations and tasks are likely to be augmented, transformed, or automated by AI, and what reskilling measures are required?
