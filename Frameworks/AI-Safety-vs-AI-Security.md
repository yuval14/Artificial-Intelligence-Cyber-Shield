# AI Safety vs AI Security

## Overview
AI Safety and AI Security are complementary but distinct disciplines.

| Aspect | AI Safety | AI Security |
|---|---|---|
| Goal | Prevent unintended harm from AI behavior | Protect AI systems from malicious attacks |
| Threat source | Accidental failures | Intentional adversaries |
| Focus | Alignment, robustness, reliability, fairness | Confidentiality, integrity, availability, resilience |
| Examples | Hallucinations, reward hacking, unsafe outputs | Prompt injection, model theft, adversarial examples, data poisoning |

## AI Safety
- Alignment
- Robustness
- Human oversight
- Explainability
- Fairness
- Reliability

## AI Security
- Secure AI supply chain
- Model and data protection
- Identity and access management
- AI red teaming
- Prompt injection defenses
- Continuous monitoring and incident response

## Key Frameworks
### Safety
- NIST AI RMF
- OECD AI Principles
- Frontier Model safety frameworks

### Security
- MITRE ATLAS
- OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications
- CSA AI Controls Matrix
- NIST AI 600-1 Generative AI Profile

## Best Practice
Organizations should implement both AI Safety and AI Security as complementary pillars of trustworthy AI.
