# LinkedIn Launch Post

I am pleased to share the **Artificial Intelligence Cyber Shield**, a public knowledge repository designed to support the secure, safe, and trustworthy adoption of artificial intelligence.

As AI systems evolve from standalone models into autonomous agents connected to tools, data, memory, APIs, and critical business processes, security can no longer be addressed as an isolated technical control. It requires an integrated approach connecting:

- AI security and safety
- AI governance and assurance
- AI threat modeling
- AI red teaming
- AI agent security
- Secure AI engineering
- AI supply-chain security
- AI incident management
- AI-enabled cyber defense

The repository brings together international frameworks, standards, threat models, engineering practices, testing methodologies, and governance guidance from organizations including NIST, ISO/IEC, ETSI, ENISA, OWASP, MITRE, the Cloud Security Alliance, OECD, CISA, NCSC, and leading AI laboratories.

Its purpose is to provide researchers, CISOs, security architects, red and blue teams, policymakers, and national-security practitioners with a structured reference for translating AI risks into practical governance and technical controls.

The project is continuously evolving. Planned additions include expanded agent-security principles, AI supply-chain guidance, framework mappings, incident case studies, decision-support tables, and APA 7 references.

Repository:
https://github.com/yuval14/Artificial-Intelligence-Cyber-Shield

Contributions, professional feedback, and suggestions for additional frameworks are welcome.
